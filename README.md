 # Relational Z₄ Causet Theory

A different take on a discrete toy universe.

## Overview

This project develops a variant of causal set theory in which the fundamental objects are directed edges carrying Z₄ phase labels, rather than bare points with a partial order. The dynamics is local: no global clock, global metric driving the dynamics. At least in the first two parts, it is integers-only and I try to give a convincing explanation of how the group closes under multiplication. 

From these ingredients — edges with phases, locality, algebraic consistency — a number of features appear to emerge: a spacetime-like split (directed structure gives time-like, undirected frustration landscape gives space-like), superposition from underdetermination, a path to a path integral-like structure, particle-like defects, charges, fields and vacuum dynamics. Insightful or just an interesting fiction? I don't know yet.

Whether the framework produces sane geometry or is congruent with other theories in an appropriate limit is open. So are many other things. This is early-stage work.

## Status

Frankly, I was too dumb to understand fully LQG. And it bothered me that if we take seriously that everything is quantized, then it seemed like starting with a Hilbert Space and SU(2). already was operating several levels above the base of reality.

Most of this project will rest on some Philosophical reasoning, informed by past Philosophers, and (for the most part) undergraduate Mathematics - basic set theory, knowledge of graphs and so forth. Holonomy creeps in later.

Whether I am right or not - I cannot say. I cannot say if it is even possible to say or what "right" even means.

## Postulates

The sanity of the universes that I care about lead to:
1. All dynamics are local.
2. Otherness and opposition are different things and this is *important*.
3. Relations comprise the universe, not things. The act of becoming and adding a new relation are the same. The related "things" are secondary. A "view" if you will, on the relation.
